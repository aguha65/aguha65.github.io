Abelmoschus moschatus L . (Malvaceae).


Hibiscus abelmoschus L.



ENGLISH NAME    Muskmallow.

Ass - Gorokhia koroi.
Ben - Maskdana.
Hin. - Latakasturi, Maskdana.
Mal. - Latakasturi.
San. - Latakasturi.
Tam. - Vettrilaikkasturi.
Tel. - Kasturibendavittu.

DESCRIPTION    
Perennial, erect, hispid, fast growing herb. Leaves, usually palmate, 3 to 7 lobes; lobes oblong, ovate, irregularly toothed, hairy on both surface. Flowers yellow, with purple center, hermaphrodite. Insect Pollinated. Fruits hairy, capsular, many seeded. Seeds black, scented[34].
Flowering and fruiting summer [9].

DISTRIBUTION    
Terrestrial. Throughout India. Range S.E. Asia - Himalayas to China and Vietnam. In Nepal at elevations of 600 - 1100 metres[84]. Flat areas, valleys, stream sides and scrub slopes in western and southern China[49].

CULTIVATION    
Easily grown in a rich well-drained soil in a sunny position[9]. Tolerates a pH in the range 6 to 7.8, is not hardy in the colder areas , it tolerates temperatures -5°c [9]. It is probably wise to apply a good mulch to the roots in the autumn,[71] and best to cut back the stems to about 15cm long in spring [9].
Seed - sow April in a greenhouse, germinates best at a temperature around 24°c[9], can also be sown in situ in late April in areas with warm summers. Cuttings of half-ripe wood, July in a frame[9].

PARTS USED    
Root. Seeds. Leaves. Seedpod.

USES    
An emulsion made from the seed is antispasmodic and is especially effective in the digestive system[56, 9]. The seeds are also chewed as a nervine, stomachic and to sweeten the breath[56, 9]. They are also said to be aphrodisiac[56, 9]. The seeds are valued medicinally for their diuretic, demulcent and stomachic, stimulant, antiseptic, cooling, tonic, carminative and aphrodisiac properties. A paste of the bark is applied to cuts, wounds and sprains[84]. The essential oil is used in aromatherapy for the treatment of depression and anxiety[9]. It is also applied externally to treat cramp, poor circulation and aching joints[9].

OTHER USES    
Essential oil is obtained from the plant[9], used as a food flavouring and in perfumery as a musk substitute[9]. Upper part of the plant, fruit, seeds are used as an insecticide[56,23,9]. Fibre is obtained from the stem bark is used to make ropes[9, 84]. A mucilage obtained from the roots is used as a size for paper[9].
Young leaves and shoots used as a vegetable[9]. The leaves are also used to clarify sugar[47]. Unripe seedpods - cooked as a vegetable. Seeds fried or roasted , has a flavour similar to sesame seeds[84], also used as a flavouring for liqueurs or to scent coffee[9,47]. An essential oil is obtained from the plant and is used to flavour baked goods, ice cream, sweets and soft drinks[47].

CONSTITUENTS    
Beta - sitostrol and its glucosides, higher fatty acids, eaters of acetic acid, ambrettolide, farnesol, linoloic acid.

STATUS    
Wild and well cultivated.
