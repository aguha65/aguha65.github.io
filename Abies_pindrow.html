Abies pindrow - Royle. (Pinaceae)


Abies webbiana var. pindrow - (Royle.)Brandis.



ENGLISH NAME    West Himalayan Fir

Bhu. - Dumshing.
Lep. - Dumshing.
Nep. - Gobra salla.
San. - Talispatra.

DESCRIPTION    
Evergreen tree growing at a slow rate. Flowers are monoecious (individual flowers are either male or female, but both sexes can be found on the same plant) and are pollinated by wind.
Seeds ripen from October to November.

DISTRIBUTION    
E. Asia - W. Himalayas from Afghanistan to W. Nepal, at altitudes between 2000 - 3000 metres [145, 158].

CULTIVATION    
Prefers a good moist but not water-logged soil[46]. Grows well in heavy clay soils. Plants are very shade tolerant, especially when young, but growth is slower in dense shade[115]. Intolerant of atmospheric pollution[46]. Prefers slightly acid conditions down to a pH of about 5[68]. Prefers growing on a north-facing slope[68].
Seed - Sow early February in a greenhouse or outdoors in March[120]. Germination is often poor, usually taking about 6 - 8 weeks[120].

PARTS USED    
Leaf

USES    
Leaf carminative, expectorant, stomach pain, tonic, astringent , used in asthma and bronchitis. It is anti pyretic. Used in hoarse voice, internal haemorrhage and tuberculosis. Gum mixed with oil of roses, when taken internally, produces intoxication and externally used for headache and neuralgia.

OTHER USES    
Wood - light, soft, not very durable used for house interiors, cases, furniture, water troughs and fuel[136, 122, 57]. Ink prepared out of cones after boiling.

STATUS    
Terrestrial, wild and cultivated.
