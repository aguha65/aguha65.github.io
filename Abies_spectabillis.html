Abies spectabilis. (D.Don.) Spach. (Pinaceae).


Abies webbiana. Lindl.
Pinus spectabilis - D.Don.



ENGLISH NAME    Himalayan Fir.
Gar. - Chirao, Bang.
Hin. - Talispatra.
Kas. & Dongri - Badar.
Kum. - Raisalla.
San. - Talisa, Talisapatra.

DESCRIPTION    
Evergreen, monoecious tree. Young shoots hairy, bark blackish, shallow, fissured. Leaves needle like, spreading above in all directions[61]. Male cones solitary or in distant pairs, erect. Flowers are monoecious (individual flowers are either male or female. Both sexes can be found on the same plant) and are pollinated by wind. Seeds winged, wing longer than the seeds.
Seeds ripen from October to November.

DISTRIBUTION    

E. Asia - Himalayas from Afghanistan to Nepal, Bhutan, Pakistan, Mayanmer, Tibet, at an altitude 2100 to 3900m in India [88]. Generally occurring in cool and moist situation with deep rich soil.

CULTIVATION    
Prefers a good moist but not water-logged soil [46]. Grows well in heavy clayee soils. Plants are very shade tolerant, especially when young, but growth is slower in dense shade [115]. Intolerant of atmospheric pollution[46]. Prefers slightly acid conditions down to a pH of about 5[68]. Prefers growing on a north-facing slope[68]. Trees should be planted into their permanent positions when they are quite small, between 30 and 90cm in height. Plants are strongly outbreeding, self-fertilized seed usually grows poorly[68]. They hybridize freely with other members of this genus[68].
Seed sown early February in a greenhouse or outdoors in March[120]. Germination is often poor, usually taking about 6 - 8 weeks[120]. Stratification is said to produce a more even germination so it is probably best to sow the seed in a cold frame as soon as it is ripe in the autumn[85, 39]. The seed remains viable for up to 5 years if it is well stored[39].


PARTS USED    
Leaves.Wood.

USES    
Leaves are astringent, carminative, expectorant, stomachic and tonic[23]. Leaf juice used in the treatment of asthma, bronchitis etc. [23, 88, 84]. An essential oil obtained from the leaves is used to treat colds, rheumatism and nasal congestion[84]. The leaf juice is antiperiodic[23, 88].

OTHER USES    
Essential oil is obtained from the plant [23]. Dried leaves, mixed with other ingredients, used in making incense[84]. Wood is used for construction and thatching roofs, and as fuel[84].

CONSTITUENTS    
Essential oil and an alkaloid, biflavonoid, abiesein.

STATUS    
Terrestrial, wild and cultivated.
