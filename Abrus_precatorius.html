TRIBAL NAME    Kauch, Ara-kuch, Ara-kee.

DESCRIPTION    
A much-branched, slender, perennial, deciduous, woody, prickly twing or climbing herb. Stem cylindrical, wrinkled; bark smooth textured, brown. Leaves , abruptly pinnate; leaflets-10 to 16 pairs, oblong, rounded at both ends, thinly embranous; rachis ending in a spine. Inflorescence one-sided pedunculate raceme. Flowers purplish-white in flowering shoot. Pollination by insect. Fruits oblong, pods are turgid with the sharp deflexed beak. Seeds red with dark black spot or sometimes -pure white.
Flowering and fruiting August to September.

DISTRIBUTION    
Darjeeling - Sevoke, Kalijhora, up to 1000m[33].

CULTIVATION    
Propagated by seeds.

PARTS USED    
Root, leaf, seed.

USES    
Root, tonic, diuretic, emetic, alexiteric, also used in preparation for gonorrhea, jaundice, Leaves, decoction cough & cold, colic pain, skin diseases (leaf juice).
haemoglobinuric bile. Leaves and roots as substitute for liquorices in cattaral affections. Seeds purgative, emetic, tonic, antiflogistic, aphrodisiac, used in nerves disorder. Also used as abortifacient. Seed paste stiffness of soldiers joint and paralysis. In veterinary medicine, it is used in the treatment of fracture.

OTHER USES    
The seeds are said to consistently weigh 1.75 gm and used by Indian jewelers as lowest unit of weight (rati), also used to make necklace.

CONSTITUENTS    
Root & leaves - glycyrrhizin, precole, abrol, abrasine, precacine.
Seeds - gallic acid, abrine, hypaphorine, allanine, serine, valine, cholin, trigolline, precatorine, methyl ester, cholanic, abrin & abtrin.[34]

STATUS    
Frequent

CAUTION    
Seeds contain poisonous active principle known as abrin.
Bruised seeds used criminally for poisoning cattle and for homicidal purposes.
